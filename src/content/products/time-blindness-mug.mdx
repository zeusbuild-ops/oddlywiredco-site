Time blindness isn't bad time management. It's a fundamentally different relationship with time itself.

Most people experience time as a continuous, even-paced thing. You know how long an hour feels because it feels the same as the last hour. Tomorrow morning feels close because the gap between now and then is a thing you can estimate.

ADHD time runs on two settings. "Not yet" — which means it could be five minutes away or three days away, and you genuinely cannot tell the difference. And "oh no" — which is the moment "not yet" snaps directly into "this thing was supposed to happen seven minutes ago and you are still in your pyjamas."

There is no in-between. No 30-minute warning. No "this will take a while, better start now" feeling. It's a switch, not a dial.

So this mug names it. Two speeds. That's the whole system.

Drink the coffee while you're still in "not yet." That's when the morning is still soft and you've got time. By the time it's "oh no," the coffee is cold and you're in the car.

We see you. Welcome.
