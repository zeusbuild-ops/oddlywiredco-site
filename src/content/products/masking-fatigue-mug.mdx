There's a thing that happens around 3pm on a Tuesday where you realise you've been performing the version of yourself that other people find easiest for about six hours straight. The version that smiles when you're confused. The version that says "yeah, makes sense" when nothing in the meeting made sense. The version that responds to small talk like a normal human and not someone whose internal monologue is a fire alarm.

That version of you costs something. We just don't have a great word for what.

This mug calls it: mask battery. And it's running low.

Masking isn't fake. It's not lying. It's the social armour that most undiagnosed (and a lot of diagnosed) ADHD/AuDHD people put on to get through a world that doesn't really fit. And like any battery, it runs out. Usually right when you need it most.

So this is a permission slip in mug form. A small reminder, every morning, that the version of you behind the mask is the real one. The mask is the costume.

Take it off when you can.

(Available in 11oz. UK-printed by T Shirt and Sons. Dishwasher safe but hand-wash if you want it to last forever, which honestly all of us do.)
